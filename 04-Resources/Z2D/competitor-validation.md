## Slide 1 — Competitor Landscape: The Gap Is the Reporting Front Door

### Headline
**Facility software exists — but the everyday reporting front door is still too heavy.**

### Table

| Segment | Example players | What they already solve | Gap Fridson exploits |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise IWMS / CAFM / EAM** | Planon, IBM TRIRIGA, ServiceNow, Eptura | Broad workplace, asset, maintenance, service, field-service, and IoT workflows. Planon covers integrated workplace management, asset maintenance, field services, service management, and IoT support[^1](https://www.planonsoftware.com/us/software/planon-asset-maintenance-management/). | Powerful but heavy. Not built as a zero-login, everyday reporting layer for every employee, tenant, cleaner, or visitor. |
| **Mobile CMMS** | MaintainX, UpKeep, Limble, Fiix, eMaint | Mobile work orders, asset history, preventive maintenance, QR codes, photos/notes, technician workflows. MaintainX says QR scans can retrieve asset history and create new work orders[^2](https://www.getmaintainx.com/blog/optimizing-maintenance-with-qr-codes-and-a-cmms). | Still primarily a maintenance-team system. Fridson is the lightweight capture layer before a ticket becomes a work order. |
| **QR / photo-first task tools** | Snapfix | Fast photo, video, voice, and QR-code work orders; Snapfix positions around the “3-second work order”[^3](https://www.snapfix.com/). | Closest direct competitor. Fridson must win on smarter routing: in-house vs contractor, severity, asset context, and escalation. |
| **Intelligent FM platforms** | Infraspeak, Facilio | Connect assets, teams, suppliers, compliance, work orders, mobile apps, and predictive intelligence. Infraspeak positions around breaking the “reactive loop” and connecting FM ecosystems[^4](https://www.infraspeak.com/). | Strong platforms, but broad. Fridson can be installed as the fast QR/reporting wedge without replacing the whole FM stack. |
| **Predictive / IoT maintenance** | TMA, Maximo, Siemens/Brightly, Fracttal, Augury | Sensor data, condition monitoring, thresholds, automated work orders, MTTR/MTBF tracking. Predictive maintenance best practices emphasize vibration/temperature/pressure thresholds and automated CMMS/EAM workflows[^5](https://www.tmasystems.com/blog/predictive-maintenance-best-practices). | Requires sensor coverage, integration, and historical data. Fridson starts with human-reported structured events, then adds sensors. |

### Bottom-line positioning
**Fridson is not another full CMMS. It is the zero-friction building reporting layer: scan, tap, and the right responder gets exact asset/location context instantly.**

### Speaker note
“Incumbents validate the market. Our wedge is the missing front door: today issues start as vague emails, hallway comments, WhatsApp messages, or clunky forms. We make the building itself reportable.”

## Slide 2 — Validation: The Pain Is Real, and the Wedge Is Credible

### Headline
**Maintenance reporting breaks before maintenance even starts.**

### 4 sourced validation bullets

1. **Large, growing budget category**  
   Facility management software is estimated at **$2.98B in 2026**, growing to **$4.95B by 2031** at **10.68% CAGR**[^6](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/facility-management-software-market).  
   **Takeaway:** There is real budget for software that improves building operations.

2. **Current reporting is messy and low-quality**  
   Facility maintenance requests often arrive through “hallway conversations, sticky notes, phone calls, and emails,” creating missed jobs and unclear priorities[^7](https://www.getmaintainx.com/blog/facility-maintenance-examples-7-cmms-success-stories).  
   **Takeaway:** The first mile of reporting is a real bottleneck, not just a workflow preference.

3. **Practitioners already validate QR reporting**  
   A facility-management Reddit user said service email “works OK” but tenants “leave out vital detail information”; another user described implementing a QR flow that logs building, area, and fault into Excel[^8](https://www.reddit.com/r/FacilityManagement/comments/1mnej2o/would_a_qrbased_issue_reporting_tool_save_you/).  
   **Takeaway:** Teams are already duct-taping this workflow because the pain is acute.

4. **Predictive maintenance is attractive — but needs data first**  
   Predictive maintenance in buildings faces data scarcity/cold-start issues and building-specific integration challenges[^9](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7913483/).  
   **Takeaway:** Fridson’s QR layer creates structured asset-event data now, enabling predictive maintenance later.

### Optional proof metric if you need a fifth bullet
QR-based maintenance sources claim QR requests can reduce reporting time dramatically and cut asset misidentification by pre-loading exact asset/location context[^10](https://oxmaint.com/article/qr-code-maintenance-requests-report-equipment-issues). Treat this as vendor-sourced, so use it as directional support rather than the main proof point.

### Bottom-line validation statement
**The market already buys facility software. Users already complain about missing context. Operators already hack together QR flows. Fridson packages that behavior into a polished, AI-routed workflow.**

### Speaker note
“We are not asking judges to believe that buildings need maintenance software. That market is established. We are asking them to believe the first step is broken: reporting is too vague, too manual, and too slow. Our live demo fixes that exact first step.”
